Welcome to Tahiti

The Heart of French Polynesia

Tahiti, the largest island in French Polynesia, is a captivating blend of lush landscapes, vibrant culture, and serene beaches. Dominated by volcanic mountains, cascading waterfalls, and verdant valleys, the island offers a picturesque backdrop for both adventure and relaxation. The capital city, Papeete, serves as a bustling hub of activity, featuring lively markets, cultural landmarks, and a fusion of French and Polynesian influences.

Beyond its natural beauty, Tahiti is rich in history and tradition. From ancient marae (temples) to colonial-era architecture, the island tells stories of its past through well-preserved sites and museums. The local population, known for their warm hospitality, adds to the island’s charm, making every visitor feel welcomed and embraced by the spirit of “mana”—the life force that permeates Polynesian culture.

Whether you’re seeking thrilling adventures, cultural immersion, or tranquil moments by the sea, Tahiti offers a diverse array of experiences to satisfy every traveler’s desires.

Things To Do

Papenoo Valley 4x4 Safari

Embark on a guided off-road adventure through the lush Papenoo Valley. Discover hidden waterfalls, ancient archaeological sites, and panoramic mountain views as you delve into Tahiti’s verdant interior.

Fautaua Waterfall Hike

Trek through the Fautaua Valley to reach one of the world’s tallest waterfalls, cascading nearly 300 meters into a serene pool. The hike offers a blend of natural beauty and historical intrigue, passing by the ruins of Fort Fachoda.

Robert Wan Pearl Museum

Explore the world’s only museum dedicated to pearls. Learn about the history, cultivation, and significance of Tahitian pearls, and view exquisite pieces from Robert Wan’s private collection.

Musée de Tahiti et des Îles

Delve into Polynesian culture at this ethnographic museum in Puna’auia. Exhibits showcase traditional artifacts, historical photographs, and insights into the islands’ rich heritage.

Notre Dame Cathedral, Papeete

Admire the oldest Catholic church in Tahiti, featuring a blend of European and Polynesian architectural elements. The cathedral stands as a testament to the island’s colonial history.

Le Marché de Papeete

Immerse yourself in local culture at this vibrant market. Shop for handcrafted souvenirs, fresh produce, and traditional Polynesian garments while experiencing the daily life of Tahitians.

Botanical Gardens of Papeari

Stroll through expansive gardens showcasing Tahiti’s diverse flora. The serene environment offers a peaceful retreat and a chance to learn about native plant species.

Vaipahi Gardens

Explore these beautifully landscaped gardens featuring waterfalls, walking trails, and a variety of tropical plants. The site also holds cultural significance with ancient marae located within the grounds.

Boat Tour

Step aboard the Tahiti Boat Tour Pofai and unlock the wonders of Bora Bora’s legendary lagoon from the heart of Pofai Bay. Glide across crystalline turquoise waters on a private or small-group excursion, where every moment is tailored for discovery and delight.
